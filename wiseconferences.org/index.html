OUR GOAL

Foremost objective of the WiSE Conference is to inspire girls in High Schools and Colleges across India to develop a deep understanding of the power of technology and how it can help to solve various real world problems and bring these solutions to life through prototypes.

Why WiSE

According to a report published by the Ministry of Human Resources and Development in the year 2013 - 2014 called the All India Survey on Higher Education (AISHE), a total of about 25.5 Million students enrolled for Undergraduate Programs of Study in various disciplines. Out of this population of 25.5 Million students, only 3.9 Million students enrolled for Bachelor of Engineering and Bachelor of Technology Undergraduate Programs. This means that only 15% (3.9 Million of 25.5 Million) of the total number of students that pursued Undergraduate Programs in India pursued courses related to Technology and Engineering. Further, out of this population of 3.9 Million students that enrolled in Technology and Engineering Programs, 2.8 Million were Male and 1.1 Million were Female. This means that only 28% of the total number of students that opt for Technology and Engineering Programs at the Undergraduate level in India are Female.

WHAT WILL THE STUDENTS DO AT WiSE

The students will use technology and applications of computing technology. We will teach them about various existing and upcoming technologies. With this knowledge and using the Design Thinking Cycle, students will design and prototype solutions for real world problems.

Who will Students work with

Students will work with key experts from the fields of Safety, Healthcare, Fitness & Fashion. Upcoming studios include Waste Management, Water Management, Alternative/Renewable Energy, Transportation & Infrastructure. These experts come from varied backgrounds and bring a diverse set of knowledge and skills ranging from Engineering Design, Software development, and Understanding of Electronics. All the studios will be supported by a Mentor specific to that studio. The Mentors will share their expertise about their field with the students. This will help students develop understanding about the challenges in the field and the use of technology to address the problem. They will help students to design relevant prototype solutions using Design Thinking.

Design Thinking

Design Thinking is an approach to design that uses specific techniques and mindsets to develop creative solutions. Identifying problems and generating solutions for design challenges allows students to frame their thinking around developing empathy, promoting a bias toward action, encouraging ideation, and working in a culture of prototyping. We believe technology driven solutions aren't the work of lone geniuses. They are the result of skilled collaborative teams systematically working to understand human needs and building on each other's knowledge, thinking and skills to design solutions. The design thinking process is key for guiding and supporting this collaboration and innovation.

Projects

Relaxo is a dress that has an inbuilt massager and a headphone that can be used by students during and after school to relax. This was created by students at WISE 2016.

Safety Jacket that can tell a construction worker about any falling debris so that they can avoid such accidents. This was created by students at WISE 2016.



Light Cap can be used as a flashlight at night and even look good and fashionable. This was created by students at WISE 2016.



SOS Bracelet will help girls who need safety while travelling alone such that they can get to travel and explore new things. This was created by students at WISE 2016.



Angels of DJHS is a service that aims to help middle aged people by reducing suffering caused from hypertension. This device alse helps them maintain a healthy diet, get regular exercise and enough rest through regular alerts. This was created by students at WISE 2016.



Road Safety wearable device that will alert the pedestrian beforehand if the car is coming in their way so that they can be safe while they are walking or crossing the road. This was created by students at WISE 2016.
