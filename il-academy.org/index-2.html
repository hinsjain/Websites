Leading School Innovation

The new reality, the irony, and the promise for school change is that schools need to become the agile learning organizations that develop and utilize the same skills that their students need to develop and utilize.

Why?

Disruptive Change

In the World Economic Forum’s 2016 report on the future of work, organizations across a wide range of industries identified “insufficient understanding of disruptive change” as the leading barrier to change that is faced by their organizations (World Economic Forum, 2016). Organizations and industries ranging from communications and entertainment, to agriculture and retail, to healthcare and the military to education, all require the capacity to understand the disruptive change environment to generate insights and take action for relevant change.

Understanding disruptive change in order to generate insights and then take informed actions starts with understanding the major long-term trends that have been and will continue to drive global change.

Drivers for Innovation

The Institute for the Future (IFTF) identifies six major long-term trends that are driving change in our world and redefining the context and purposes of education.

Those drivers are:

Extreme Longevity

Increasing global lifespans change the nature of careers and learning.

Rise of Smart Machines

Workplace automation nudges human workers out of rote repetitive tasks.

Computational World

Massive increase in sensors and processing power make the world a programmable system.

New Media Ecology

New communication tools require new media literacies beyond text.

Superstructred Organizations

Social technologies drive new forms of production and value creation.

Globally Connected World

Increased global interconnectivity puts diversity and adaptability at the center of organizational operations.

In-Demand Skills

Drivers help us understand the emerging shape and direction of global change. They point us toward the skills our students need to develop and utilize in order to cope and flourish in their dynamic contexts. Many of these skills like sensemaking, complex problem solving, social skills, and transdisciplinary learning, aren’t new to schools. Other skills like virtual collaboration, new-media literacy, design thinking and managing cognitive load are new to schools. Their common link is their increasingly fundamental necessity and utility for everyday life.

Innovation Leadership Academy

Creating adaptable schools and adaptable students requires leaders to develop skills and capacities to lead school innovation. Consilience has created the Innovation Leadership Academy to support educators to lead innovation at their schools.

What is the Innovation Leadership Academy?

The Innovation Leadership Academy develops educators’ skills and capacities to lead school innovation and transform teaching and learning. We provide action oriented workshops and training where educators learn to lead innovation through creating authentic innovations for their schools and students.

What are the offerings of the Academy?

We develop and offer a variety of workshops and training such as Design Thinking, Prototyping, Trends Literacy & Forecasting, Learning through Social Entrepreneurship, Technology Integration, Learning through Making, Essential Conditions for School Innovation. We also offer the following Innovation Leadership Certificate program. It is a three-part training where participants develop innovation leadership capacity through authentic, hands-on innovation creation in a school.

Leading Change through Making and Tinkering

This one day training is for school leaders interested in using Maker pedagogy, tools, and practices to build a culture of Innovation at their schools. Participants will learn the fundamentals of building a Maker Culture, learn strategies to get members of their community involved, and develop a plan of action to get started.

Innovation Leadership Certificate

Create new innovations for your school and build your innovation capacity to lead! Innovation Leadership Academy offers a two day Certificate for school leaders who want to learn how to grow their innovation capacity through creating school innovations designed to meet the needs of their students and impact their schools.

Part 1 - Accelerate Your Change

  • Design Thinking Skills & Processes
  • Prototyping Skills & Processes

The Certificate is designed to equip school leaders with the knowledge and skills required to create new innovations for their students at their schools. Participants learn a systematic approach to identifying important learning opportunities and designing prototypes customized for their unique school contexts. Participants customize individual plans for leading their prototypes at their schools. Participants take away guides and templates as well as the custom prototypes and action plans they have designed to impact student learning at their schools.

Part 2 - Develop Your Essential Conditions

  • Establishing & Growing and Essential Conditions for successful innovation

The Certificate equips school leaders with the knowledge and skills necessary to establish and grow a culture for innovation at their schools. Participants learn about the Essential Conditions for successful school innovation through their experience leading prototypes they designed during Part 1. Participants learn how to grow, develop, and leverage the different Essential Conditions to support specific prototypes and foster a culture of innovation in their schools. Participants take away tools, resources, and custom action plans for strategically establishing and growing the Essential Conditions at their schools.

Optional

Participants are encouraged to tell the story about leading innovation and write and publish a chapter about their innovation journey in think. make. learn., an open source international journal of school-based innovation.
